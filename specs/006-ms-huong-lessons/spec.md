## Book Overview (Source Material)

The source PDF ("Sach_Tieng_Anh_Ms_Huong_Thiet_Ke.pdf", 73 pages) is a Vietnamese English pronunciation course structured in 5 parts:

| # | Vietnamese | English |
|---|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Học tổng quát 43 âm | General overview of 43 sounds |
| 2 | Luyện từ | Word practice |
| 3 | Luyện câu | Sentence practice |
| 4 | Luyện bài | Text/passage practice |
| 5 | Phụ lục 500 Vocabulary (Tự học thêm) | Appendix: 500 vocabulary (self-study) |

**Part 1** (pages 3–26) contains pre-lesson phonetics reference content including 3 challenge exercises with word lists and IPA phonetics, plus visual charts (consonants, vowels, diphthongs). The word lists are text-extractable.

**16 Lessons** (pages 27–62) each focus on a specific sound or sound group. Each lesson spans 2–3 pages, the content of which is predominantly visual/image-based (cannot be text-extracted). The title and sound focus of each lesson are extractable.

**Advanced Pronunciation** (pages 65–72) is a supplementary section after lesson 16 covering vowel/consonant comparisons and notes on combining sounds. Content is also image-based.

## Clarifications

### Session 2026-05-29

- Q: Should lesson JSON be hand-authored or generated by a parse script? → A: Hand-author the static JSON file (data is fixed, small, and the PDF is image-heavy).
- Q: How should the 32 sessions be displayed — dropdown selector or full indexed list? → A: Full list — all 32 sessions visible at once, grouped by lesson.
- Q: Should challenge word entries include a SpeakButton for audio pronunciation? → A: Yes — SpeakButton next to each challenge word, for parity with vocabulary pages.
- Q: What label convention for the two sessions within each lesson? → A: `Day N` (e.g., Day 1, Day 2 … Day 32) — consistent with `/vocabulary/500` and `/vocabulary/1000`.

### Session 2026-05-29 (revision — image content recovered)

The first pass treated the lesson teaching content as unrecoverable because the lesson pages are image-based. That dropped the actual content (alphabet chart, sound charts, per-lesson practice-word tables). The PDF's embedded page images were subsequently extracted with `pdfjs` (`scripts/extract-lesson-images.ts`) and the missing data recovered. Revised decisions:

- Q: How to preserve the image-based lesson/chart content without losing information? → A: **Hybrid** — embed the original page images per lesson/section (lossless), PLUS transcribe the 4 foundational sound charts into structured, speakable data.
- Q: Which content scope is in? → A: 16 lessons + Part 1 phonetic charts + Advanced Pronunciation.
- The 4 transcribed speakable charts: Alphabet (26), Consonant sounds (24), Monophthong vowels (11), Diphthong vowels (8) — each with a `SpeakButton`.
- All 16 lesson bodies, the Part 1 reference charts, and the Advanced section are embedded as their original PDF page images (served from `public/lessons/`).

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 — Browse Sessions by Lesson (Priority: P1)

A learner opens `/lessons`, sees all 32 study sessions organized by lesson, and can identify which lesson each session belongs to and what sounds it covers.

**Why this priority**: This is the MVP — the minimum content structure that replaces the "Coming soon" placeholder with something useful. Every other story builds on this overview.

**Independent Test**: Navigate to `/lessons`; verify all 32 sessions are visible on the page simultaneously (no dropdown required); verify sessions are grouped by lesson (16 groups of 2); verify sessions 1–2 map to Lesson 1 (Alphabets, Day 1 / Day 2) and sessions 31–32 map to Lesson 16 (/tʃ/ and /dʒ/, Day 31 / Day 32).

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the learner visits `/lessons`, **When** the page loads, **Then** all 32 study sessions are displayed simultaneously — no dropdown or selector required to reveal them.
2. **Given** a session entry is visible, **When** the learner reads it, **Then** they can see the day label (e.g., "Day 5"), parent lesson number (1–16), lesson title, and sound focus.
3. **Given** the learner refreshes the page, **When** the page loads again, **Then** the same full list is shown (no persistence needed).

---

### User Story 2 — Course Structure Overview (Priority: P2)

A learner can see the 5-part course structure of Ms. Huong's book as context for the 32 sessions, understanding how lessons fit into the broader curriculum.

**Why this priority**: The 5-part structure (Tổng quát → Luyện từ → Luyện câu → Luyện bài → Phụ lục) is important metadata from the source book. It helps the learner understand where they are in the course arc.

**Independent Test**: On the `/lessons` page, verify the 5 course parts are displayed (with Vietnamese and English names); verify Part 1 is labeled as pre-lesson foundational content; verify that the 16 lessons are associated with the relevant parts.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the learner visits `/lessons`, **When** they scan the page, **Then** the 5 course parts are visible as section headers or labels accompanying the session list.
2. **Given** the learner reads Part 1's label, **When** they look at the description, **Then** they understand it covers the foundational 43-sound overview (pre-lesson content, not one of the 16 numbered lessons).

---

### User Story 3 — Pre-Lesson Challenge Word Lists (Priority: P3)

A learner can view the three challenge word lists from Part 1 of the book — including each word, its IPA phonetic, and a SpeakButton — as a reference before starting lesson 1.

**Why this priority**: The three challenge exercises (Đọc số, Đọc từ khá giống nhau, Đọc từ phổ biến) are the only lesson-adjacent content that is fully text-extractable from the PDF. Surfacing them preserves information that would otherwise be invisible on the page. Lower priority because the session browser alone is a usable MVP.

**Independent Test**: On the `/lessons` page (or a dedicated sub-section), verify the three challenge sections are visible: "Challenge 1 — Đọc số" (10 number words with IPA + SpeakButton each), "Challenge 2 — Đọc từ khá giống nhau" (8 similar words with IPA + SpeakButton each), "Challenge 3 — Đọc từ phổ biến" (24 common words with IPA + SpeakButton each).

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the learner opens the challenges section, **When** they view Challenge 1, **Then** they see 10 rows each showing the ordinal number, English word (e.g., "One"), IPA phonetic (e.g., /wʌn/), and a SpeakButton that pronounces the word.
2. **Given** the learner views Challenge 2, **When** they read the list, **Then** they see 8 similar-sounding words (like, life, line, lime, lie, light, liar, lice) each with IPA and a SpeakButton.
3. **Given** the learner views Challenge 3, **When** they read the list, **Then** they see 24 commonly mispronounced words each with IPA and a SpeakButton.
4. **Given** the learner clicks any SpeakButton, **When** the browser supports Web Speech API, **Then** the English word is pronounced aloud.

### Edge Cases

- What happens when a lesson has IPA symbols that don't render in the chosen font? The IPA phonetics (e.g., /ʃ/, /ŋ/, /θ/, /ð/) must display correctly — the page must use a font that supports the full IPA character set.
- How is "Advanced Pronunciation" (post-lesson-16 section) handled? It is supplementary, not a numbered lesson, so it appears as a labeled note after Lesson 16 (not as sessions 33–34).
- What if the learner's viewport is very narrow (mobile)? The session list must be readable without horizontal scrolling at 375 px wide.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

- **FR-001**: The page at `/lessons` MUST display all 32 study sessions simultaneously (no dropdown selector) — all sessions visible on page load without interaction.
- **FR-002**: Sessions MUST be grouped by lesson (16 groups of 2 sessions each). Each session entry MUST show: day label (`Day N`), parent lesson number (1–16), lesson title, and sound focus (IPA notation).
- **FR-003**: The page MUST present the 5-part course structure of the source book (Vietnamese and English names) as contextual navigation or labeling.
- **FR-004**: The page MUST include the three Part 1 challenge word lists with their IPA phonetics (10 + 8 + 24 entries respectively), with a SpeakButton next to each word.
- **FR-005**: All IPA phonetic symbols MUST render correctly and legibly on both desktop and mobile.
- **FR-006**: The page MUST be viewable at viewport widths from 375 px and above without horizontal scrolling.
- **FR-007**: The page chrome MUST be consistent with the existing `/vocabulary/500` and `/vocabulary/1000` pages: shared navigation sidebar, breadcrumb path, ASCII/terminal aesthetic, monospace typography.
- **FR-008**: All lesson, session, and challenge data MUST be stored as a hand-authored static JSON file (no database, no parse script). The data is fixed and small (16 lessons, 32 sessions, 42 challenge words).
- **FR-009**: The "Advanced Pronunciation" supplementary section MUST be acknowledged on the page (e.g., a brief note after Lesson 16) — it must not be silently omitted.

### Key Entities

- **LessonEntry**: Represents one of the 16 lessons — fields: lesson number (1–16), title (English), sound focus (IPA string), part of the book (1–5), page range in source PDF.
- **SessionEntry**: Represents one study session — fields: day number (1–32, displayed as "Day N"), parent lesson number, lesson title (denormalized for display), sound focus (denormalized for display).
- **ChallengeEntry**: One word in a Part 1 challenge exercise — fields: challenge number (1–3), ordinal within challenge, word (English), IPA phonetic.

## Assumptions

- The 16 lessons are the primary navigable content; the 5 course parts serve as labels/context only (no separate part pages).
- Sessions are labeled `Day 1` through `Day 32`: Lesson 1 → Day 1 & Day 2, Lesson 2 → Day 3 & Day 4, …, Lesson 16 → Day 31 & Day 32. This convention matches the vocabulary pages.
- The split within each lesson is even (½ lesson per session) with no prescribed content boundary — Day 1 and Day 2 of a lesson are both shown with identical lesson metadata (title + sound focus).
- The Advanced Pronunciation section (pages 65–72) is supplementary, not a 17th lesson, so it is displayed as a note rather than generating sessions 33–34.
- Part 5 (Phụ lục 500 Vocabulary) refers to the 500-word vocabulary already handled by `/vocabulary/500` — it is referenced on the page but not duplicated.
- Lesson content pages are image-based; their content is preserved by **embedding the extracted page images** per lesson (lossless), alongside title + sound-focus metadata and the 4 transcribed speakable sound charts. (Superseded the original assumption that this content could not be recovered.)
- The existing VT323 monospace font supports standard Latin and most common IPA glyphs; for any IPA symbol outside the font's coverage, a fallback system font stack will supply the glyph (no special font loading needed for the spec phase).
- No user session progress, bookmarking, or persistence is in scope — the page is a read-only reference browser.
- All lesson, session, and challenge data will be hand-authored into a static JSON file at `src/data/lessons/ms-huong-lessons.json`. No parse script is needed.
